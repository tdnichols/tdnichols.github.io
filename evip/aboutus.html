About eVIP

The Electronic Vendor Invoicing Program (eVIP) is an exciting initiative to transition NIH from paper to electronic invoicing!

eVIP is based on Oracle iSupplier, a secure web-based electronic invoicing and payment information system. Electronic invoice submission through iSupplier:

  • Enables faster processing of invoices
  • Gives you access to real-time information on the status of your orders, funding, invoices and payments
  • Reduces the risk of cancelled invoices

How Do I Get Involved?

We are currently in a pilot phase. If you are a pilot vendor and have already received an invitation to register for eVIP, please click on the link in your invitation to initiate the registration process. If you are interested in participating in the next phase of the pilot, please contact the eVIP Help Desk.

How Does Registration Work?

To join eVIP, your organization must be invited by NIH and then register. NIH extends the initial invitation to your organization’s Electronic Business Point of Contact (EBPOC). Your EBPOC(s) are listed in SAM.GOV and we recommend you coordinate with your EBPOCs to verify that they are correctly listed and that they are prepared to help register your organization. Once an EBPOC receives an invitation and registers, he/she can invite other users in your organization to register as well.

Registration is a 5–10 minute online process that involves verifying your personal identity (for NIH IT security purposes), and payment of an annual user fee. Each user in an organization must go through this payment and identity verification process before gaining access to NIHâ€™s invoicing system, Oracle iSupplier. Go to the Verizon registration guide for more information about the registration process.

Once registered for eVIP, there are two types of access offered in Oracle iSupplier. The first is “view-only” which allows you to conduct real-time research about orders and invoices. The second is “invoice-entry” which allows you to submit invoices electronically and perform the same research functions as the “view-only” access.
