eVIP Frequently Asked Questions

Invoice Status

How do I look up the status of an invoice?

Log in to iSupplier, and select the Search field drop-down from the top of the Home Page. Select Invoices and enter your invoice number in the Search field. Select Go. You can also research invoices by selecting the Invoices link from the Quick Links section, or by selecting the Account tab at the top right corner of the page or the Account link at the bottom of the Home Page.

Who should I contact if my invoice is on hold?

If your invoice is on hold, please contact your Contracting Officer, Buyer or IC Vendor Support. The contact information is available in iSupplier by using the “Find My CO/Buyer/IC Vendor Support” search tool.

Who should I contact if I have invoice questions?

You can use the “Find My CO/Buyer/IC Vendor Support” search tool in iSupplier to find contact information for invoice status-related questions about holds. For any other invoice related questions, contact the eVIP Helpdesk.

Registration

Why do I need to set up a user profile?

The creation of a user profile will permit you to be issued a personal, secure password to verify your identity each time you attempt to log in. The delivery of this password to you will confirm your identity and grant you access your personal information; making sure that someone else is not trying to access your account.

Why do I need another password to log in?

By requesting an additional password, a significantly higher level security can be provided to you and your personal information when you log in. By using a second password delivered to contact information that you provided, a higher level of assurance can be established that you personally are logging in, as opposed to an unauthorized person attempting to gain access your account and information.

Why do I need to provide you with my contact information?

Personal, secure passwords can be delivered to you in a variety of ways, based on the information you submit. These methods can be a voice call to one of your phone numbers, a text message sent to a cell phone you possess or provided directly to you by your smart phone, if you installed the mobile application.

Why are you asking for multiple contacts?

It is recommended that you enter several different contacts in your profile. A password will be sent to you each time you attempt to log in so that your identity can be confirmed; so please be sure to enter contacts that you will have access to, when you to typically log in.

As an example, if you work from home it is recommended to enter your cell phone and your home phone number, either of which you can specify to receive your password. If you typically use your cell phone to receive your password and the cell phone battery is dead, or you lose it; you can request your password to be sent to your home phone (through an automated call) and still be able to log in.

Why do I need to create a PIN?

If you are using the mobile application, you will need to enter this PIN number to gain access to the application and use it as the delivery mechanism to receive your personal, secure password.

Why are you asking for my personal information?

Personal information like the last 4 digits of your SSN, your birth month and year and/or your current address is used to definitely verify your identity, so that you can be granted the appropriate and secure access when you log in.

Verizon employs a variety of techniques and services to verify your identity. One of the tools used to confirm your identity requires the verification of antecedent data or data that is the result of historical transactions and events specific to you. In order to accurately locate your historical data records used in the identity verification processes, the last four digits of your Social Security number and the month and year of your birth are used to ensure we verify you against your historic data records.

Note: Please be sure to enter accurate information, as it is requested during the registration process, failure to do so could limit your access or prevent you from logging in.

How is my identity verified?

The registration process ensures the existence of your identity per comprehensive industry standards. Please note that not all the data requested may be necessary to verify your identity. The verification process stops once your identity has been verified. Hence some data for some users may not be checked.

Regardless, all users registered via Universal Identity Services (UIS) satisfy the highest standards. Specifically, Universal Identity Services is verified by the Federal Identity, Credentialing and Access Management (FICAM) program, as a level 2 & 3 issuer for non-PKI credentials and certified by Kantara, which is a certified FICAM Trust Framework Provider.

Why do I need to answer security questions?

If you are ever unable to access your account (if for example you only have a cell phone as a contact and you lose it), you will be asked these security questions to confirm your identity before you are permitted to access your account and user profile. Therefore, when you answer the questions, please be sure to answer clearly and in a way that you will remember the specific responses you entered.

Is the data I provide secure?

Yes. Verizon manages this service and has technical, administrative and physical safeguards in place to protect against unauthorized access to, use or disclosure of all personal information maintained by UIS, especially your personally identifiable information, such as your government issued identifiers.

Why did my social security number and/or date of birth verification fail?

The Universal Identity Service utilizes you name, SSN, and month/year of birth information provided during the application process to access your historical data records used to verify your identity. If for some reason UIS is not able to retrieve your records, please check to ensure that your name, Social Security number, and month/year of birth were entered correctly.

While registering, I didn’t get the one-time passcode. What do I do?

Select another method/contact and try again. You can use either the same method for receiving the code or try a different method, such as email, home, work or cell phone.

What should I do if my account is deactivated?

If your account is deactivated, please identify the email code at the bottom of the deactivation alert and contact the Helpdesk.

Do I need to download any software to use this service?

No, for processing of you payment we do not require any additional software. For the Universal Identity Services ID, that will be provided after your payment has been processed, you will have access to downloadable software.

Is the eVIP Payment Portal a secure web site?

When you enter information online, the information will be scrambled using SSL encryption technology before being sent over the Internet. Although we cannot guarantee encryption or the privacy of your data, SSL makes it very difficult for your data to be stolen or intercepted while being transferred. When we receive your data, it is kept encrypted until we’re ready to process it.

How will you share my personal information?

For the payment portion of this service all financial transaction information occurs through PayPal. The PayPal privacy policy is available here.

Payment

Is there a fee for this service that I will have to pay?

Yes. The eVIP Payment Portal will require an annual fee in order to support the cost of the Universal Identity that has been provided to you.

If I am unwilling to pay this service fee, can I still use this service?

No. The service fee covers the cost of providing a unique ID for processing NIH invoices and is not negotiable.

Will I be automatically charged an annual renewal fee?

No, you will be sent reminder emails approaching the end of the annual period about renewing your membership.

Can I cancel a payment once it has completed?

No. If you make a mistake with the payment, you should contact the NIH helpdesk. Please review all requirements of the eVIP service carefully before you accept and submit payment for the service. Once transactions have been submitted, they cannot be canceled or changed.

Is my payment processed immediately?

Yes. Your credit card is charged immediately. Once the transaction has settled with your credit card and we receive the funds, the payment is sent to the company providing this service for NIH. Please allow seven days for your payment to be received.

What do I do if I am having trouble with the payment portal or viewing the pages?

Please contact the eVIP helpdesk.

Who should I contact if the transaction is declined?

You should contact the bank or financial institution that issued the card.

Who should I contact if there is a billing error or I have a question about fees charged to my credit card or PayPal account?

You should contact the bank or financial institution that issued the card or account.

Passwords and Login

I forgot my password. How do I find or reset it?

If you forget your Verizon UIS password, you’ll have to reset it. Select the “Forgot Password?” link from the Home tab. The system will guide you through the steps for resetting your password. You will be asked security questions as part of these steps and will need to provide the correct answers to the questions.

I forgot my username. How do I find or reset it?

If you saved your initial account setup email from Verizon, your username is located in that email. If you don’t have this email, you can use your email address to login IF you registered your email account as one of your contacts to receive one-time passcodes. Once you have logged into the profile manager, you can find your username at the top right corner of the eVIP home page.

What username and password do I use for iSupplier Login and BizAdmin Login?

You use the password for your Verizon Universal Identity Services (UIS) identity.

How many times can I try entering my password before it is locked?

Passwords can be tried three (3) times before they are locked and need to be reset.

How do I reset my password?

Enter your email address and verify your identity by successfully answering your security question. Then, enter the email address where you’d like the one time password to be sent. Finally, enter your new password (the system will automatically take you back to the NIH website) where you can then login to iSupplier.

What are the Password guidelines/rules?

A password must contain all of the following:

  • At least 1 lower case letter
  • At least 1 upper case letter
  • 1 special character (e.g. “$”, “%”, “!”, “?”, etc.)
  • 1 number

While registering, I didn’t get the one-time passcode. What do I do?

Select another method/contact and try again. You can use either the same method for receiving the code or try a different method, such as email, home, work or cell phone.

I entered the wrong one-time passcode. What do I do?

Select “OK” in the error message window and try again. You can use either the same method for receiving the code or try a different method.

I received an error message about an invalid email address or password. What do I do?

Select “OK” to close the error message window. Then, be sure that your CAPS LOCK key is off and try again. If you are still experiencing difficulty, please contact the HELP DESK.

I received an error message that the host is unavailable. What do I do?

Select “OK” to close the error message window. Then, check your Internet connection. The most likely problem is that your computer is not connected to the Internet. An internet connection is required in order to perform the identity check. On rare occasions, the Verizon UIS system might be down for maintenance.

What should I do if my account is deactivated?

If your account is deactivated, please identify the email code at the bottom of the deactivation alert and contact the Helpdesk.

Oracle iSupplier

What is Oracle iSupplier?

Oracle iSupplier is the system that is integrated with NIH’s core accounting system and enables NIH’s Electronic Vendor Invoicing Program (eVIP). It is a Web-based portal for vendors to electronically request payment and monitor payment status.

What are some of the benefits of using Oracle iSupplier?

Oracle iSupplier enables more efficient and accurate processing of payments, as well as real time monitoring and tracking of invoices. The system also:

  • Reduces manual payment processing time by enabling electronic approvals, as well as streamlining the integration of invoices with NIH’s core accounting system;
  • Provides e-mail notifications on all invoice approvals and rejections, as well as real time information about the status of invoices; and
  • Enables the uploading of electronic attachments to provide supporting documentation for invoices and credit memos.

Will there be training on how to use Oracle iSupplier?

NIH provides training materials on this website under the Training Tab including comprehensive Web-based training, videos, frequently asked questions (FAQs), and other resources to assist your organization.

My organization invoices multiple NIH Institutes and Centers (IC). Will all ICs have the same policies and procedures?

Standard policies and procedures for the new electronic invoicing process are currently in place, although you should always follow the guidance in your awards and from your Contract Officer.

How will my organization provide back-up documentation for my invoice?

Oracle iSupplier will allow vendors to electronically attach any required documentation to each individual invoice. Vendors will no longer need to mail documentation to the NIH, though it is recommended that vendors still wishing to maintain a hard copy record print the documents they have uploaded to Oracle iSupplier to ensure proper version control.

What is Invoice Entry?

Invoice Entry is a capability in iSupplier that enables vendors to create invoices, match them to their correct purchase order, and submit them to NIH electronically. Vendors can also research the status of their invoices by viewing purchase orders, invoices and payment status.

What should I do if my account is deactivated?

If your account is deactivated, please identify the email code at the bottom of the deactivation alert and contact the Helpdesk.

BizAdmin

What is NIH Registration and Authentication or NIHRA?

NIH Registration and Authentication (NIHRA) is an NIH portal that allows a vendor’s designated BizAdmins to manage their organization’s eVIP users.

What/Who is a BizAdmin?

BizAdmin is a role that certain designated individuals in your organization have. BizAdmins are able to invite other users in their organization, manage iSupplier access for those users, and designate additional BizAdmins.

Is there a limit to how many people in my organization can register for eVIP?

No, there is no limit. There is a $29.95 +tax annual fee per user that is paid to Verizon to create and maintain your secure login credentials.

Is there a limit to how many BizAdmins my organization can register for eVIP?

No, there is no limit. Any BizAdmin can designate another individual to be a BizAdmin. An organization’s first BizAdmin is the Electronic Business Point of Contact (EBPOC).

What is an Electronic Business Point of Contact (EBPOC)?

Electronic Business Points of Contact (EBPOCs) are registered by organizations that conduct business with the Federal Government in GSA’s System for Award Management (www.SAM.gov). NIH’s eVIP invitation process begins with an invitation to a vendor’s EBPOC.

What should I do if my account is deactivated?

If your account is deactivated, please identify the email code at the bottom of the deactivation alert and contact the Helpdesk.

General

What is eVIP?

The NIH’s Electronic Vendor Invoicing Program (eVIP) is a secure, web-based electronic invoicing and payment information program. It consists of two systems: Oracle iSupplier (iSupplier) for invoice entry and viewing, and NIH Registration & Authentication (NIHRA) which allows vendors who are designated BizAdmins to manage their organization’s eVIP users. Access to these systems requires an identity verification and a login credential that is managed by Verizon Universal Identity Services (UIS).

How do I sign up for eVIP?

Participation in eVIP starts with an invitation from NIH to register. This initial invitation is extended to a vendor’s Electronic Business Point of Contacts (EBPOCs), listed on www.SAM.gov. Once a vendor’s EBPOC registers for eVIP, he or she can invite additional users in their organization. eVIP is currently in pilot phase. Additional vendors will be invited to participate in 2014. To express interest, please contact the eVIP Helpdesk. If you are a pilot vendor and have been invited to participate in eVIP Pilot Phase 2, please click on registration link in your invitation email to begin the registration process.

My organization does business with several NIH Institutes, Centers & Offices. Which invoices will I see?

All your invoices can be viewed in Oracle iSupplier, the enabling application for eVIP.

When can I submit invoices to NIH using eVIP?

eVIP is currently in pilot phase. Additional vendors will be invited to participate in 2014. To express interest, please contact the eVIP Help Desk.

Glossary of eVIP Terms and Acronyms

Award

The provision of funds by NIH based on a solicitation and offer and a mutually binding agreement between the NIH and an organizational entity or individual to provide supplies or services.

Acquisition

The acquiring by contract with appropriated funds of supplies or services (including construction) by and for the use of the Federal Government through purchase or lease, whether the supplies or services are already in existence or must be created, developed, demonstrated, and evaluated. Acquisition begins at the point when agency needs are established and includes the description of requirements to satisfy agency needs, solicitation and selection of sources, award of contracts, contract financing, contract performance, contract administration, and those technical and management functions directly related to the process of fulfilling agency needs by contract.

Blanket Purchase Agreement (BPA)

A simplified method of filling anticipated repetitive needs for supplies or services by establishing “charge accounts” with qualified sources of supply including Federal Supply Schedule and open market Vendors. The BPA establishes no contractual obligation on either party to buy or sell. A BPA Call is the mechanism used to place orders and obligate funds.

Buyer

A role responsible for creating awards in PRISM to vendors responsible for providing goods/services to the NIH. Contact information of NIH Buyers associated with specific orders/awards is available for viewing in iSupplier.

Contract

A mutually binding legal relationship obligating the seller to furnish the supplies or services (including construction) and the Buyer to pay for them. It includes all types of commitments that obligate the Government to an expenditure of appropriated funds and that, except as otherwise authorized, are in writing. In addition to bilateral instruments, contracts include (but are not limited to) awards and notices of awards; job orders or task letters issued under basic ordering agreements; letter contracts; orders, such as purchase orders, under which the contract becomes effective by written acceptance or performance; and bilateral contract modifications. Contracts do not include grants and cooperative agreements covered by 31 U.S.C. 6301, et seq. For discussion of various types of contracts, see FAR Part 16.

Contracting Officer

A role responsible for reviewing and approving awards in PRISM to vendors for providing goods and services to the NIH.Â Contact information of NIH Contracting Officers associated with specific NIH orders/awards is available for viewing in iSupplier.

Delivery Order

A contract for supplies that does not procure or specify a firm quantity of supplies (other than a minimum or maximum quantity) and that provides for the issuance of orders for the delivery of supplies during the period of the contract. See related “Order” term also.

Data Universal Numbering System (DUNS)

The 9-digit number assigned by Dun and Bradstreet, Inc. (D&B), to identify unique business entities, which is used as the identification number for Federal contractors.

Electronic Business Point of Contact (EBPOC)

Electronic Business Points of Contact (EBPOCs) are registered by organizations that conduct business with the Federal Government in GSA’s System for Award Management (www.SAM.gov). NIH’s eVIP invitation process begins with an invitation to a vendor’s EBPOC. Once registering with NIH for eVIP, they can in turn invite other users in their organization to participate in eVIP.

eVIP

NIH Electronic Vendor Invoicing Program. eVIP is NIH’s electronic invoicing solution that is enabled by the Oracle iSupplier application. The program is designed to provide a shared, real-time view of invoice payment status to OFM, ICs, and vendors; allows vendors to enter and match invoice electronically; provides capability for paperless, more accurate invoicing.

IC

Institutes and Centers (of the NIH).

IDIQ

Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity. The Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR), section 16.501(a), outlines a type of federal contract that provides for an indefinite quantity of supplies or services during an indefinite period of time.

Invoice

A contractor’s bill or written request for payment under the contract for supplies delivered or services performed.

iSupplier

Oracle financial application that allows vendors to enter and match invoices electronically. The application also provides a shared, real-time view of invoice payment status and vendor information for OFM, ICs and suppliers. See related “Oracle” term also.

NIH Business System (NBS)

Replaces the Administrative Database (ADB).A single, integrated, transaction-based system. The NBS integrates the NIH administrative and scientific support functions, enterprise human resources and payroll, budget, acquisition, finance and travel.

National Institutes of Health (NIH)

A part of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, is the nation’s medical research agency-making important discoveries that improve health and save lives.

OALM

OALM provides leadership, guidance and oversight for acquisition and logistics at NIH.

OFM

OFM provides central accounting and reporting for all financial transactions that originate from the 27 Institutes and Centers (ICs). OFM also provides overall direction and leadership to the ICs by establishing financial management policies and procedures and by providing oversight of the NIH Core Financial Management Systems.

Oracle

An object-relational database management system produced and marketed by the Oracle Corporation. See related “iSupplier” term also.

Prior Period Receipts

If an item was physically received during a prior accounting period (i.e., the previous month), but receiving is not entered into Oracle iProcurement until a subsequent accounting period, the Receiver must also record the actual receipt date into the system using the Prior Period Receipts function.

PRISM

Procurement Request Information System Management. Managed by Compusearch Software Systems, the PRISM is used to record and track requisitions, purchase orders and contracts issued by offices and councils within the Executive Office of the President. The system has an interface with the Oracle financials system called the Oracle Compusearch Interface (OCI).

Procure-to-Pay Process

Business processes that cover activities of requesting or requisitioning, purchasing, receiving, paying for and accounting for goods and services.

Prompt Payment Act

Public Law 97-177, which states that each Federal agency who acquires services, supplies or equipment from Vendors and does not make timely payment for such delivery by the required payment date shall pay an interest penalty to the Vendor. Purchase Order When issued by the Government, the terms means an offer by the Government to buy supplies or services, including construction and research and development, upon specified terms and conditions, using simplified acquisition procedures. See related “Order” term also.

Requisition

An official request that describes the goods/services that an NIH Institute or Center is requesting.

SAM.GOV

System for Award Management. SAM (www.SAM.gov) is the official U.S. Government system that consolidated the capabilities of CCR (Central Contractor Registry)/FedReg (Federal Agency Registration), ORCA (Online Representations and Certifications Application), and EPLS (Excluded Parties List System).

Task Order

A contract for services that does not procure or specify a firm quantity of services (other than a minimum or maximum quantity) and that provides for the issuance of orders for the performance of tasks during the period of the contract. See related “Order” term also.

Tax Identification Number (TIN)

The number required by the IRS to be used by the offeror in reporting income tax and other returns. The TIN may be either a Social Security Number or an Employer Identification Number.

Three-Way (3-way) Matching Process

The process of verifying that the information contained in an award, electronic receiving, and vendor invoice match in order to make invoice payments.

Two-Way (2-Way) Matching Process

The process of verifying that the information contained in cost-type awards match in order to make invoice payments. 2-way vendor invoices require approval from the Contract Involve Approver in order for OFM to process payment.

Vendor

Any individual or other legal entity that may potentially do business with the Federal Government. Upon award of a contract, the vendor becomes a “contractor.” See related “Contractor” term also.
